Lesson 1: Exploring Personal Narratives

Your Speaking Guides

Personal narratives are powerful tools for sharing your experiences, dreams, and perspectives. When telling your story, remember to:

  • Use vivid details to paint a picture for your audience
  • Connect your experiences to emotions and lessons learned
  • Organize your thoughts chronologically or by importance
  • Practice speaking clearly and at an appropriate pace
  • Make eye contact and use natural gestures when presenting

Example: "This was the most beautiful place I have ever visited."
Wish and If Only: For expressing desires about travel
Example: "I wish I could travel to Japan next summer."

Lesson 14: Leadership and Teamwork

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing leadership and teamwork:

  • Share specific examples from personal experience
  • Discuss both challenges and successes
  • Emphasize collaboration and communication skills
  • Consider different leadership styles
  • Reflect on lessons learned from group work

Grammar Focus: Leadership Communication

Gerunds and Infinitives: Enjoy doing vs. decide to do
Example: "I enjoy working in teams, but I decided to take on more leadership roles."
Causative Verbs: Make, let, have, get someone to do something
Example: "A good leader can get people to work together effectively."
Expressing Ability: Can, could, be able to, manage to
Example: "She was able to resolve the conflict between team members."

Lesson 15: Future Goals and Aspirations

Your Speaking Guides

When discussing future goals:

  • Be specific about your goals and timelines
  • Explain the steps you'll take to achieve them
  • Discuss potential challenges and solutions
  • Connect your goals to your values and interests
  • Show enthusiasm and determination

Grammar Focus: Future Aspirations and Plans

Future Perfect: Will have + past participle
Example: "By 2030, I will have completed my master's degree."

Example: "I hope to make a positive impact on society."
